GOVERNMENT SCHEMES FOR URBAN CIVIC IMPROVEMENT — INDIA
=======================================================

OVERVIEW
---------
The Indian government runs several flagship schemes aimed at improving urban infrastructure, sanitation, water supply, and the overall quality of life in cities and towns. Citizens can benefit from and contribute to these schemes.

1. SWACHH BHARAT MISSION — URBAN (SBM-U)
-----------------------------------------
Launched: 2014 (Phase 1); 2021 (Phase 2)
Objective: Making India open defecation free (ODF) and achieving clean cities

Key Initiatives:
- Construction of individual household toilets (IHHL)
- Community and Public Toilets (CPT)
- Solid Waste Management (SWM)
- Liquid Waste Management (sewage treatment)
- Behavioural change campaigns
- ODF++ and Water+ city certification

How Citizens Can Participate:
- Download Swachh City App and rate cleanliness
- Report dirty spots / open garbage through the app
- Participate in shramdaan (voluntary cleaning drives)
- Apply for household toilet subsidy if you don't have one

Helpline: 1969
Website: swachhbharaturban.gov.in

---

2. AMRUT — ATAL MISSION FOR REJUVENATION AND URBAN TRANSFORMATION
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Launched: 2015 (Phase 1); 2021 (AMRUT 2.0)
Objective: Water supply and sewerage infrastructure in cities with population above 1 lakh

Key Focus Areas:
- Universal water supply coverage (piped water to all households)
- Underground sewerage coverage
- Storm water drainage
- Urban mobility & green spaces
- Smart metering for water

Beneficiaries: 500 cities in Phase 1; expanded in Phase 2

How Citizens Can Benefit:
- Apply for water and sewage connections through the city corporation
- Track project progress on city corporation dashboards

Website: amrut.gov.in

---

3. SMART CITIES MISSION
------------------------
Launched: 2015
Objective: Develop 100 smart cities with sustainable urban development

Key Components:
- ICT-enabled governance (e-governance, smart surveillance)
- Integrated Command and Control Centres (ICCC)
- Smart traffic management
- Smart streetlights with sensors
- Wi-Fi in public spaces
- E-waste management
- Smart water meters and distribution

Current Status: 100 cities selected; most have functional ICCCs
Complaint Mechanism: Through city-specific smart city apps and portals

Website: smartcities.gov.in

---

4. PRADHAN MANTRI AWAS YOJANA — URBAN (PMAY-U)
-----------------------------------------------
Launched: 2015
Objective: Housing for All — affordable housing for urban poor

Eligibility:
- EWS (Economically Weaker Section): Annual income up to ₹3 lakh
- LIG (Low Income Group): ₹3–6 lakh
- MIG I: ₹6–12 lakh
- MIG II: ₹12–18 lakh

Benefits:
- Subsidy on home loan interest (CLSS component)
- Ready houses for urban slum dwellers
- Affordable housing projects under PPP mode

Apply at: pmaymis.gov.in / city corporation offices
Helpline: 1800-11-6163

---

5. PM SVANidhi — STREET VENDOR ATMANIRBHAR NIDHI
-------------------------------------------------
Launched: 2020
Objective: Micro-credit and formalisation for street vendors

Benefits:
- Working capital loan of ₹10,000 (initial), ₹20,000, ₹50,000
- Digital transaction incentive
- Social security benefits
- Formalisation through vendor certificate

Apply at: pmsvanidhi.mohua.gov.in
Helpline: 1800-11-0001

---

6. DEEN DAYAL ANTYODAYA YOJANA — NATIONAL URBAN LIVELIHOODS MISSION (DAY-NULM)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Objective: Reducing poverty and vulnerability of urban poor households

Components:
- Social mobilisation through Self Help Groups (SHGs)
- Skill training and placement
- Self-employment through micro-enterprise support
- Shelter for urban homeless

---

7. NATIONAL URBAN HEALTH MISSION (NUHM)
-----------------------------------------
Objective: Health care for urban poor

Services:
- Urban Primary Health Centres (UPHC)
- Mobile Medical Units
- Free medicines at government facilities
- Janani Suraksha Yojana (safe motherhood)

---

8. RIGHT TO SERVICE ACT
------------------------
Available in states: Most major Indian states have enacted this
Key Provisions:
- List of government services must be delivered within stipulated time
- Penalty on officials who delay services
- Appellate mechanism for citizens

Services Covered: Water connection, birth/death certificates, trade licences, building permits, sanitation complaints, etc.
