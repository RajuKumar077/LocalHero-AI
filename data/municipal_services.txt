MUNICIPAL SERVICES IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
====================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Municipal corporations in India (also called Nagar Nigam, Bruhat Mahanagara Palike, or Greater Municipal Corporation depending on the city) are responsible for providing essential civic services to urban residents. They are governed by elected councillors and administered by the Indian Administrative Service (IAS).

SERVICES PROVIDED BY MUNICIPAL CORPORATIONS
--------------------------------------------
1. Water Supply and Distribution
2. Sanitation and Solid Waste Management
3. Road Construction and Maintenance
4. Street Lighting
5. Drainage and Sewerage Systems
6. Public Parks and Gardens
7. Birth and Death Registration
8. Building Plan Approvals
9. Trade Licences
10. Property Tax Collection
11. Public Health Services
12. Primary Education (Municipal Schools)
13. Fire and Emergency Services

HOW TO REGISTER A COMPLAINT
-----------------------------
Citizens can report civic issues through multiple channels:

a) Online Portals:
   - MyGov (mygov.in) — national portal for citizen grievances
   - Swachh Bharat Mission (swachhbharaturban.gov.in) for sanitation issues
   - Individual city corporation websites (e.g., bbmp.gov.in, mcgm.gov.in, mcd.gov.in)

b) Mobile Applications:
   - Swachh City App — for garbage, drainage, and sanitation complaints
   - BBMP Sahamati App (Bengaluru)
   - MyBMC App (Mumbai)
   -311 App (many cities)

c) Phone Helplines:
   - National Municipal Helpline: 1533
   - Swachh Bharat Helpline: 1969
   - Local ward office contact (available on corporation website)

d) In Person:
   - Visit your local Ward Office
   - Speak to your elected Ward Councillor
   - Approach the Zonal Commissioner's office for escalation

COMPLAINT TRACKING
------------------
All registered complaints receive a unique Complaint Registration Number (CRN). Citizens can track status using this number on the respective portal or by calling the helpline.

ESCALATION HIERARCHY
---------------------
Level 1: Ward Inspector / Junior Engineer
Level 2: Assistant Commissioner (Ward)
Level 3: Zonal Commissioner
Level 4: Commissioner (Municipal Corporation)
Level 5: State Urban Development Department
Level 6: Chief Minister's Public Grievance Portal

RIGHTS OF CITIZENS
------------------
Under the Right to Services Act (enacted in most Indian states), citizens have the right to:
- Receive civic services within stipulated time limits
- Get a written acknowledgement of complaints
- Appeal if service is not delivered in time
- File grievances without fear of retaliation
