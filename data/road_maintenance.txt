ROAD MAINTENANCE DEPARTMENT — SERVICES, CONTACTS & COMPLAINT PROCEDURES
========================================================================

DEPARTMENT OVERVIEW
--------------------
Road maintenance in Indian cities is managed by multiple agencies depending on the type of road:
- National Highways: National Highways Authority of India (NHAI)
- State Highways: Public Works Department (PWD) of the state
- City Roads & Arterial Roads: Municipal Corporation (Roads & Bridges Department)
- Internal Colony Roads: Resident Welfare Associations + Corporation
- Village Roads: Gram Panchayat / Rural Development Department

SERVICES PROVIDED
-----------------
1. Pothole repair and road resurfacing
2. New road construction
3. Road widening and divider construction
4. Footpath / pavement construction and repair
5. Traffic signal installation and maintenance
6. Road marking and signage
7. Flyover and underpass maintenance
8. Stormwater drain (alongside roads) maintenance
9. Street lighting installation (in coordination with Electricity Dept)
10. Removal of encroachments on roads

COMMON ROAD ISSUES
------------------
1. Potholes on road — MEDIUM to HIGH (depending on size and traffic)
2. Large crater/sinkhole — CRITICAL (immediate safety risk)
3. Road cave-in after rain — CRITICAL
4. Damaged footpath / broken pavement — MEDIUM
5. No street lighting on road — HIGH (safety at night)
6. Missing manhole cover — CRITICAL (risk of accident/fatality)
7. Encroachment on footpath/road — HIGH
8. Road waterlogging after rain — HIGH
9. Damaged road divider — MEDIUM
10. Illegally broken road for utility work (not restored) — HIGH

HOW TO REPORT ROAD ISSUES
---------------------------
Phone:
- NHAI Helpline (National Highways): 1033
- PWD State Roads: Contact your state PWD helpline
- Municipal Corporation Roads: Check city corporation website
- National Road Safety Helpline: 1033

Online:
- Pothole reporting: Many cities have dedicated apps
  * Mumbai: pothole.mcgm.gov.in
  * Delhi: mcdonline.nic.in
  * Bengaluru: BBMP Sahamati App / bbmp.gov.in
- PM Grievance Portal: pgportal.gov.in
- MyGov: mygov.in

In Person:
- Ward Engineer's Office of your Municipal Corporation
- Nearest PWD Sub-Division Office (for state highways)

RESOLUTION TIMELINES
---------------------
- Pothole (small, < 1 ft): Repair within 7 days
- Pothole (large): Repair within 3 days
- Missing manhole cover: Emergency replacement within 24 hours (critical safety risk)
- Road cave-in / sinkhole: Emergency barricading within 2 hours; repair within 24 hours
- Footpath repair: Within 15 days
- Street light: Within 3–5 working days

REPORTING TIPS FOR CITIZENS
-----------------------------
When reporting a road issue:
1. Note the exact location: nearest landmark, street name, ward number
2. Take a clear photograph of the issue
3. Measure or estimate the size (for potholes)
4. Note if there have been recent rain events (helps diagnosis)
5. Keep your CRN (Complaint Registration Number) safe for follow-up
