WATER SUPPLY DEPARTMENT — SERVICES, CONTACTS & COMPLAINT PROCEDURES
====================================================================

DEPARTMENT OVERVIEW
--------------------
The Water Supply Department (called BWSSB in Bengaluru, MCGM Water in Mumbai, DJB in Delhi, Chennai Metro Water in Chennai, HMWS&SB in Hyderabad) is responsible for providing safe drinking water and managing sewerage networks across the city.

SERVICES PROVIDED
-----------------
1. 24x7 or scheduled piped water supply to households
2. Water tanker supply (when pipe supply is interrupted)
3. New water connection applications
4. Sewerage connection and maintenance
5. Maintenance of underground drainage (UGD) network
6. Borewell and groundwater regulation
7. Water quality testing
8. Replacement of broken water meters

COMMON WATER SUPPLY ISSUES
----------------------------
1. No water supply / dry taps — HIGH priority
2. Low water pressure — MEDIUM priority
3. Water leakage from main pipeline on road — HIGH priority (wastes water + damages road)
4. Contaminated / coloured water — CRITICAL (public health risk)
5. Overflowing manholes/sewerage — CRITICAL (health hazard)
6. Broken / damaged water meter — MEDIUM priority
7. Illegal water connection — HIGH priority
8. Burst water pipeline — CRITICAL (immediate repair needed)
9. Water logging due to broken sewerage — HIGH priority

RESOLUTION TIMELINES
---------------------
- No water supply: Water tanker must be sent within 24 hours; pipe supply restoration within 48 hours
- Water leakage from main: Repair within 24 hours
- Burst pipeline: Emergency response within 4–6 hours
- Contaminated water: Investigation within 24 hours; resolution within 48 hours
- New water connection: 15–30 working days
- Water meter replacement: 7–10 working days

WATER QUALITY
--------------
The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) IS:10500 specifies drinking water quality standards. If water appears brown, smells unusual, or causes illness, report immediately to the water board AND the public health department.

Emergency Water Contamination Reporting:
- State Pollution Control Board
- District Collector's office
- Public Health Engineering Department

WATER CONSERVATION TIPS
------------------------
- Fix leaky taps immediately — a dripping tap wastes up to 20 litres per day
- Reuse RO reject water for mopping and gardening
- Report illegal borewell drilling to the Central Ground Water Board
